10 Best Twitter Accounts about Value Investing to Follow on X in 2024

Date: 2024-04-25

In the world of investing, value investing stands out as a strategy focused on finding stocks that appear underpriced relative to their intrinsic value. Social media, especially Twitter, has become a vital platform for sharing insights and strategies, making it easier than ever to access advice from leading experts. If you’re looking to deepen your understanding of value investing, here are the 10 best Twitter accounts to follow on X (formerly known as Twitter).

1. Turtle Bay (@turtlebay_io)

Turtle Bay dives deep into business history to pull out insightful investment cases. This account is ideal for those who appreciate learning from the past to inform future investment decisions.

2. Guy Spier (@GSpier)

Guy Spier’s account offers a personal touch, reflecting his life as a father, husband, and seasoned investor. His tweets provide a blend of personal wisdom and professional insights into value investing.

3. Mohnish Pabrai (@MohnishPabrai)

A renowned author and investor, Mohnish Pabrai shares insights that are as profound as his books, “The Dhandho Investor” and “Mosaic.” His account is a treasure trove for anyone serious about the fundamentals of value investing.

4. Michael Mauboussin (@mjmauboussin)

As an adjunct professor at Columbia Business School, Michael Mauboussin brings an academic perspective to value investing. His feed is rich with detailed analyses and educational resources.

5. William Green (@WilliamGreen72)

Author of “Richer, Wiser, Happier,” William Green shares narratives that inspire and educate investors. His account is perfect for those looking to gain insights into the lives and strategies of the world’s top investors.

6. Brad Kaellner (@bkaellner)

Brad Kaellner focuses on the concept of “compounding on the shoulders of giants,” offering content that helps followers build wealth over time through smart, principled investing.

7. David Park (@investingdpark)

David Park’s Twitter feed is filled with notes on investing and life, providing a holistic view of value investing as a lifestyle rather than just a technique.

8. Finding Compounders (@FindingCompounders)

If lifelong learning and continuous improvement in investing are your goals, Finding Compounders is an essential follow. This account focuses on the ever-evolving nature of investment strategies.

9. Investing with Tom (@TomInvesting)

Tom talks about stocks in a straightforward, accessible style. If you’re new to value investing or looking for clear, actionable advice, his insights are invaluable.

10. Tren Griffin (@trengriffin)

Tren Griffin, who works for Microsoft, brings a unique perspective to investing. His tweets often blend technology insights with core value investing principles, offering a unique twist on traditional methods.

Why Follow These Accounts?

These Twitter accounts offer a range of perspectives on value investing from seasoned investors and industry experts. Whether you’re a beginner looking to understand the basics or an experienced investor seeking deeper insights, following these accounts will enrich your investing journey. By engaging with their tweets, you not only keep up with current trends but also build a solid foundation in the time-tested principles of value investing.
